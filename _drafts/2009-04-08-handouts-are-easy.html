--- layout: post title: Handouts are Easy date: '2009-04-08T23:03:00.004-04:00' author: B. T. Forsythe tags: - poem modified_time: '2009-04-09T01:21:27.780-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2806030340477734957.post-6762434800474619045 blogger_orig_url: http://poetry.btforsythe.io/2009/04/handouts-are-easy.html --- she runs on a platform of handouts
puts dispensers on the street

corners blending into decay
with fuel-injected efficiency
en route to sprawl

those migrant Viagrant poor
still refuse
handouts blind-
humming euthanasias in latte time

she offered me a handout,
all ill-worn nubs dangling bruise
bone trauma came away
with a palm full of spit

asked for a handout
and my ears rang with it

the glove without fingers is not
at fault, fingers sheared
away from handouts
come easy, if only

clammy fingers can wake
from claw dreams, remember
how they once grasped a proffered hand
