--- layout: post title: Stone musing date: '2009-07-29T13:22:00.003-04:00' author: B. T. Forsythe tags: - poem modified_time: '2009-07-30T00:30:34.428-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2806030340477734957.post-3641005457357125416 blogger_orig_url: http://poetry.btforsythe.io/2009/07/stone-musing.html --- I can sit across the table while
you ravage biryani, bask in
your introductions, rest in your palm
while you wrong-shoes-stumble
along leafy trails hoping
to trip you, taste your sole,
and remain a stone.

I can sit among spices
over-warm on the shelf, eloquently
hung above gas-flame,
taste zucchini, onions almost-minced, tomatoes
soaking up the olive oil
and remain a stone.

I can sit in a worn wooden seat,
consort with your laughing
eyes sipping coffee, be
thrown
through a doorway,
railed against,
sit smoking
at a caf√© table just outside,
pulsing
with that tireless drummer's
aftermath
and remain a stone.

I can sit, stoic, thinking stubborn
stone oblivion thoughts,
breathing
lies into my shadow, empty
object of my pity,
my derision,
my envy.
