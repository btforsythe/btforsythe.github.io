Fingerprints

Once, the furrows of my fingertips
traced your lips, sowing
everywhere I touched, fingerprints
you gathered and wound into your hair.

As our images ebbed into celluloid
cast against the well-worn screen,
we grew translucent, tricks of light
fading in the electric relentless glare,
casting no fingerprints.
